BUTTONS

3.17 INTRODUCTION

Some forms may have buttons on the form in addition to or instead of a tool bar.


All buttons should have have tool tips, and contain no icons (except for help button)


The tool tip must convey meaningful information which is not simply a duplicate of the label on the button item. See ERROR PREVENTION for more on tooltips


To differentiate between primary and secondary actions, use links for lookups and use buttons for actions.



Buttons on forms and popups should be rectangular, with a gray background. Top left align buttons in main windows and bottom right align buttons in forms/popups.


Buttons should be right justified on the bottom of the screen/form and move with the screen/form where possible


There are three button sizes to choose from: Small, Regular or Custom. The exact dimensions are defined below.



3.17.3 BUTTON & MENU PRIVILEGES

Small buttons are used within the forms, often in conjunction with another input field. For example change authority or set effectivity have small buttons to the right.


Regular buttons are the most commonly used, and appear at the bottom of the page, usually as page-level actions that take you to another page or close the form.


Custom buttons are used when a regular button is not sufficient to meet the business requirements, such as a button that exceeds the max char length. As a result, the custom buttons have a variable button width dependent on max char length, following the same padding as the other button sizes.
